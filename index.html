The Garfield Pipe

The surface level of the strip is simple. We see a man. A plain-looking man who is not named in the comic (although we know he is called Jon) with large collars symptomatic of the style back then - he clearly cares about his appearance at least a little. Hes holding a newspaper, perhaps the Chicago Sun Times, which infamously dropped “Garfield” from its pages in the 1970s before public outcry forced them to reverse that decision.

Is there a deeper meaning to this comic strip? What does the pipe represent?
